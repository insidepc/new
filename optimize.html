How to optimize my pc for gaming

1.Keep everything updated

make sure that your os and drivers are running on the latest version

2.Make sure to turn on the gaming mode

Game Mode detects these apps and restricts them from using up a lot of resources or disables them temporarily.

3.Change the power plan to high performance

Depending on the power plan you have enabled, power management can be a hit or miss This option is helpful for gaming laptop users who might want to save on battery life. But Desktop users don’t need to worry about this restriction so we can set it to high-performance mode

4.Clean Out the Registry

The registry is unknown to most people and can slow down the pc

You can use Clean master to keep your pc clean

5.Defragmenting Your PC

For Windows users search for "Defragment and Optimize Drives" in the menu then analyze and optimize your drivers

6.Disable Visual Effects

All those silky smooth animations you notice when opening or closing a window serve as good eye candy. Although they make the user experience a bit better, they can hinder gaming performance in some cases. This isn’t great for gaming as visual effects can take up a bit of the processing power. We want any resources we can get a hold of while gaming, so turning visual effects off can help you a bit. (this will mostly help people with older hardware or slower systems)

7.Disable Mouse Acceleration

This is another tweak that doesn’t impact performance in any way, shape, or form. But it does enhance the overall experience, and you’ll thank us for this one later. Mouse acceleration is a feature in Windows which moves the cursor depending on the speed of the mouse movements.
